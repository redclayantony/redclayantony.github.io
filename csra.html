Community Youth Development Strategy

CSRA Regional Development Center, Augusta, Georgia

One of my first major GIS projects involved geocoding home addresses of child abuse, teen delinquency and teen pregnancy cases and then creating heat maps to help locate Youth Development Centers for the CSRA Regional Development Center (CSRA RDC) in Augusta, GA. This is a project that I am still proud of because it led to my promotion from Technician to Analyst. I had to teach myself how to geocode the addresses and how to create the heat density raster. These maps were created in '01 and '02, but I still think they look pretty sharp.

Aside the the Youth project my main duty at the CSRA RDC was GPS collection for a DOT contract. I was responsible for updating road, sidewalk, trail and historical marker data for a 13-county area. Each month I received a list of new roads that I had to collect and while in route to these I would collect any of the other features I came across. I relied on a Trimble TSC-1 datalogger and backpack antenna with Terrasync firmware and post-processed everything using Pathfinder Office. This was a great opportunity for me to learn all about GPS technologies and to see a lot of historical places such as civil war battlefields.

Additionally, my work entailed providing map support for the more rural counties in the CSRA. I created a variety of maps including utilities, zoning classifications, street indexes and more. This was a great first job because I really learned a lot about GIS and GPS and it really set in motion my career in the GIS industry.
