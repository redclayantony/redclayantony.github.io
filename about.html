Russell Lyon

GIS Analyst & Developer

I’ve been using maps and spatial analysis to guide decision-making for over a decade. It’s been a large part of just about every job I have held since I first graduated, but I still see it as more of a tool that I use to do my job. My real passion is in conservation and natural resource management. My dream job would be managing some sort of protected area or working to rehabilitate degraded landscapes, but those jobs are few and far in between and often don’t pay. So for now I continue to work in GIS because it’s what I’m good at and it’ such a useful skill in so many different work environments.

Some things that I have learned in my career is that you don’t really have to know how to do everything. Because there are so many different tools and workflows and often different ways to create the same product, you’re never going to be able to master it all. For me it is more important to know that something is possible and then you can investigate how to make that idea come to life. Most of what I learn now-a-days I teach myself using online resources and forums. I still take the occasional training, but I learn more trying to incorporate other people’s examples into my own work. Another lesson I have learned is that if I find myself repeating the same task or a process is taking a really long time, then I’m probably not doing something right or I need to find an alternative pathway.

Most of my experience is with ESRI products, but for the last few years I have been using a lot more open-source applications like QGIS and GRASS. Almost all of my professional work is in ArcGIS, but at home I solely rely on QGIS and the power of all its available plugins (i.e. SAGA and GDAL toolkits). I found I can do everything that I need with it without the cost of ArcGIS.

Lately, I’ve been exploring web mapping and programming with Leaflet JS libraries equipped with Mapbox Tiles and OpenStreetMap data. The Kay Pacha page is an example that is still very much in process, but one that I enjoy working to improve in my spare time. Most of the programming I have learned I owe to free online courses such as Codecademy. I have a good working knowledge of JavaScript, Python and HTML/CSS but there’s always room to learn more. This portfolio and the Kay Pacha page are hosted on Github and in setting them up I have learned Git and how to create and merge branches.

Apart from GIS and my professional life I enjoy travelling, backpacking, biking and disc golf. I am fluent in Spanish thanks to living abroad and I speak some Quechua that I picked up when I lived in Bolivia. My personality is easy-going but I am dedicated to my work. I can work well alone but much prefer to work as part of a team because I enjoy receiving feedback and being inspired to do better work.

Thanks for taking the time to check out my work and get to know me a little better. If there is anything else you would like to know just shoot me an email and I will respond with my phone number so we can discuss in person.
