Chemistry Teacher

Zorzor, Liberia

DESCRIPTION OF SERVICE

Peace Corps Response Volunteer Russell Lyon started his service on August 26th, 2009 in Liberia. Following a five day orientation in Monrovia and Gbarnga, Liberia, he began work with the Ministry of Education in Zorzor City, Lofa County.

Mr. Lyon taught approximately 120 - 10th Graders, 100 - 11th Graders, and 80 - 12th Graders in the subjects of Chemistry and Environmental Science. He taught six different classes three times per week for a total of 13.5 hours. In addition, he helped to establish a chemistry curriculum tailored to prepare students for the West African Examinations Council’s Senior High School Certificate Exam.

Mr. Lyon assisted the principal with the daily operations of the school. He prepared a variety of documents to help with school’s organization and implemented a system to monitor the student’s attendance.

Mr. Lyon helped staff a community library where he catalogued books and reference materials and assisted students with their assignments.
